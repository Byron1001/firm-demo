# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Corporate event decision-makers in Malaysia — marketing directors, HR heads, C-suites, and founders planning conferences, gala dinners, product launches, exhibitions, and weddings. They are time-poor, risk-averse about their event, and need a partner they can trust implicitly. They land on the site looking for proof of capability, creative spark, and a sense that this team *gets* their brand.

## Product Purpose

Convert visitors into conversations. The site exists to demonstrate creative authority, showcase past work, and make it frictionless for corporate clients to start a dialogue. Success = inbound leads from the contact form and WhatsApp.

## Brand Personality

Playful, Bold, Creative. The voice is confident and warm — never stuffy or corporate-brochure. We speak like people who love what they do and are really good at it. The tone should feel celebratory, energetic, and creatively charged.

## Anti-references

- Generic corporate event websites with stock photo heroes and dense text blocks (Magnet Group, Above Creative — competent but visually conventional)
- Law firm or professional services aesthetics (the rebrand already eliminated this)
- "Safe" minimalism that blends in — no beige, no muted everything, no generic sans-serif in sight
- Over-designed busyness that obscures the message

## Design Principles

1. **Typography as identity** — Oversized, expressive type carries the brand. It's the first thing you see and the thing you remember. Every section should feel like a statement, not a paragraph.
2. **Vibrant confidence** — Coral, peach, deep navy. The palette is intentionally bold and warm. Never pastel, never muted. Color is used with conviction, not as decoration.
3. **Motion tells the story** — Scroll-driven animations, smooth transitions, and micro-interactions create a sense of energy and celebration. Every movement should feel purposeful and polished, never gratuitous.
4. **Playful precision** — Joyful and creative in spirit, impeccably executed. Every detail — spacing, timing, hover state — is considered. The fun comes through in the big moves, not sloppiness.
5. **Show, don't tell** — Let the work speak. Clients, events, testimonials are the evidence. Copy is punchy and confident, not explanatory.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA compliance for text contrast
- Reduced motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion`
- All interactive elements keyboard-accessible
- Form labels and error states clear and visible
- English-first with Bahasa Malaysia fallback consideration
